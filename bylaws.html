By-Laws

The following By-Laws were approved at the annual meeting on May 10, 1997

 

  • This organization shall be known as the "BILLOW BEACH ASSOCIATION" and shall consist of any or all property owners in the Hubbard and Uline plat as recorded in the Ashtabula County Records of Deeds. Others under unrecorded contracts or leases may be members subject to written consent of the record title owner.
  • The Board of Directors shall consist of President, Vice President, Secretary, Treasurer, five (5) Trustees and Incumbent Past President.
  • The President shall have charge and preside at all meetings. The Vice-President shall preside in the absence of the President.
  • The Secretary shall keep a record of all meetings. The Treasurer shall keep a record of all monies of the Association. All bills, except Insurance, Taxes and Electric bills shall all be signed and approved by at least three (3) trustees before Treasurer is permitted to pay said bills.
  • No funds of the Association exceeding three hundred dollars ($300.00) shall be spent nor obligations incurred on behalf of the Association by any member of the Association without the approval of two-thirds (⅔) of the Association members present at a special meeting or regular meeting. A notice shall be sent to all members notifying them of the meeting date and agenda requesting a return PROXY VOTE.
  • In the event that there is an EMERGENCY SITUATION (Emergency-Association property Threatening) the Board of Directors may use, with discretion, treasury funds up to but not exceeding the anticipated operating expense funds needed for the duration of the fiscal year, waiving the Three Hundred Dollar ($300.00) limitation.
  • The board of Directors by majority vote may obtain a Five Thousand Dollar ($5,000.00) loan for emergency purposes for maintaining association property.
  • A quorum to do business of the Association shall consist of three (3) or more trustees.
  • Election of Officers will be by ballot - other voting to be choice of ballot or – written verbal assent of the membership.
  • An annual meeting shall be held once a year (the first Sunday in June). At this meeting the Board of Directors and officers shall be elected to hold office for a one (1) year term, except that the President and Vice-President shall be elected for a two (2) year term.
  • All members shall be notified of meetings and shall register their names and addresses for the use of the Secretary and the Treasurer.
  • Yearly dues shall be recommended by a majority vote of the Trustees and determined by a majority vote of the Association members present at annual June meeting. Dues are payable by the first of July. Thereafter, a Fifty Dollars ($50:O0) late fee will be added to the amount due for all dues not paid in full by the due date.
  • No officer of the Association shall receive a salary except the Secretary and Treasurer (for work done) shall have his/her yearly dues reduced fifty percent (50%).
  • All funds of the Association shall be used for improvements, care of the grounds of Billow Beach and emergencies that may arise.
  • All funds of the Association shall be deposited in Banks authorized by Trustees.
  • The Board of Trustees shall audit the books prior to the first (1st) Sunday in June and report same at Annual Meeting. Two (2) or more Trustees shall conduct the audit.
  • The Secretary shall have books to keep By-Laws and Records. The Treasurer shall have books to keep Records of Receipts and Expenditures. These books and supplies shall be paid for by the Association.
  • The Speed Limit on Association Roads for all types of vehicles shall be five (5) miles per hour.
  • No movable camping equipment, boats, trailers, or unlicensed vehicles shall be permitted on Billow Beach premises for more than seventy-two (72) hours.
  • No pets belonging to renters, property owners or their guests shall be permitted on the Association property without a leash.
  • All pet owners are to clean up after their pets when they use Billow Beach property - this includes the beach.
  • Individual Association members shall be responsible for the cleanliness of the beach and properties.
  • To have a Beach Party of a group of six (6) or more persons, permission must be given by three (3) Trustees or Officers. Beach Party Rules (for the safety and protection of all) will be issued at that time.
  • Vehicles belonging to property owners and one (1) guest may be parked in Association parking areas. All additional cars are to be parked in the grass parking area south of the Association parking lot.
  • Only current paid-up members of the Association and their invited guests shall have access to the beach steps and the right to use parking areas and lake front.
  • Association Parking Lot fifteen (15) is not to be sold. lt is to remain a permanent asset of the Billow Beach Association.
  • No fences are to be erected higher than three (3) feet as not to block view of neighbors or structures.

Constitution

ARTICLE I - NAME

Section 1: This organization shall be known as the BILLOW BEACH ASSOCIATION.

 

ARTICLE II - OBJECT

Section 1: The object of this Association shall be to provide general supervision of the use and upkeep of Billow Beach allotment as a whole, to make such improvements and provide and maintain such services and benefits as shall be for the good of all members, and to have charge and maintain all common property belonging jointly to all members, including the beach, banks, and commons along the lake front.

 

ARTICLE III - OFFICERS AND THEIR DUTIES

Section 1: The officers of this Association shall consist of a President, Vice-President, Secretary, Treasurer, Immediate Past President, and five (5) Trustees. All officers are to serve as the Board of Directors.

Section 2: President and Vice-President shall hold office for Two (2) years. All other officers shall hold office for one (1) year or until their successors are elected.

Section 3: The officers collectively shall constitute a Board of Directors, which shall conduct all business, and manage and control all property of the Association, subject to the Constitution and By-Laws, and the orders and directions of the Association.

Section 4: The President shall preside at the meetings of the Association and the meetings of the Board of Directors. He shall have power to call special meetings at any time. He shall appoint a nominating committee consisting of three (3) members, and/or other special committees.

Section 5: The Vice-President shall perform the duties of the President in the President's absence. The Vice-President shall be in charge of all maintenance and with the President shall either assume or assign specific responsibilities in this area to any or all Trustees.

Section 6a: The Secretary shall record the minutes of each meeting of the Association and of the Board of Directors. He or she shall keep an accurate and up-to-date list of the members of the Association, and shall notify every member by mail of the time and place of regular and special meetings. At each annual meeting he or she shall present a report of the operations of the Board of Directors. He or she shall send out notice of the nominating committee as soon as it is appointed.

Section 6b: The Treasurer shall have charge of all funds of the Association, keep an accurate account of all money received and disbursed, and pay all bills approved by the Board of Directors. At each annual meeting he or she shall present a financial report.

Section 7a: The Board of Directors shall have full power and authority to conduct affairs of the Association and shall have the control and management of the business, funds, and property of the Association subject only to the action of the members in meeting duly assembled, except that it shall not have authority (i) to enter into any business transaction involving more than Three Hundred Dollars ($300.00) in money or property without first submitting such transaction to the members and obtaining their approval.

Section 7b: In the event there is an emergency situation (EMERGENCY THREATENING ASSOCIATION PROPERTY) the Board of Directors may use, with discretion, Treasury funds up to but not including the anticipated operating expense moneys needed for the duration of the fiscal year, waiving the Three Hundred Dollar ($300.00) limitation.

Section 7c: Vote to have the Board approve getting a Five Thousand Dollar ($5,000.00) loan for emergency purposes for-maintaining Association Property.

 

ARTICLE IV - COMMITTEES

Section 1: The Nominating Committee-shall present the slate at the annual meeting. Nominations may be added during the meeting with the consent of each nominee and the endorsement of each by three members.

Section 2: Special committees as needed shall be appointed by the President (See Sec. 4, Article III).

 

ARTICLE V - MEMBERSHIP

Section 1: The membership of this Association shall consist of those persons who own one or more lots in Billow Beach Allotment, as shown by the County real estate records. Others under unrecorded contracts or leases may be members subject to the consent of the record title owner.

Section 2: Each member shall have one vote at meetings for each Habitable Structure owned, whether occupied or not. When a Habitable Structure is owned jointly by two (2) or more persons, only one of these persons shall have a vote for each Habitable Structure.

 

ARTICLE VI - ASSESSMENTS

Section 1: Each member shall pay into the treasury of the Association his proportionate share of the funds to carry on the Association’s business.

Section 2: At each annual or special meeting the Association shall fix the amount to be assessed against each habitable structure for the following year, payable on or before July first (1st) and after July 1st a Fifty Dollars ($50.00) late fee will be added to the amount due. All payments shall be made to the Treasurer, who shall give receipt for same.

Section 3: If the obligation under section one (1) and/or section two (2) of this Article is not met, then legal action may be taken.

 

ARTICLE VII - MEETINGS

Section 1: The regular annual meeting of the Association shall be held the second (2nd) Sunday of June.

Section 2: Special meetings may be held at the call of the President or at the request of five (5) members.

Section 3: The Board of Directors shall meet at the call of the President as often as necessary for the proper transaction or business, but not less than twice annually.

Section 4: Not less than ten members shall be required to constitute a quorum for the transaction of business at a meeting of the Association. Members unable to attend may vote by proxy, but proxies shall not be counted in making a quorum.

 

ARTICLE VIII - ORDER OF BUSINESS

Section 1: At annual meetings the order of business shall be as follows:

  1. Roll Call of Officers
  2. Reading of minutes and action thereon
  3. Financial Report
  4. Report of Trustees
  5. Unfinished Business
  6. Election of Officers (if at this meeting)
  7. Action on Assessments
  8. New Business
  9. Adjourn Meeting

 

ARTICLE IX. AMENDMENTS

Section 1: This Constitution may be amended and/or revised at any regular annual meeting of the Association by an affirmative vote of three-fourths (3/4ths) of the members present, provided notice of such action has been given to all members.
