The Billow Beach Annual Meeting 2015

The President's Report

Accepted Minutes

Minutes from 2014's annual meeting were reviewed and accepted.

 

Vacant Restaurant

Joe reported on the status of the vacant restaurant. The current owner has secured a five year lease with new restaurant operators. This will be a Mexican restaurant. The new operators have several other restaurants and the owner is looking forward to success with the new tenants.

 

Deck Construction

During 2014 a deck was constructed at the bottom of the stairs. This has proven to be a great addition enjoyed by all. Joe thanked Gary and Paul for their contribution in completing this project. The main steps were stained by Lisa and Ryan Bednarik. Ron Zelenak helped with getting the materials for this project as well.

 

Access Steps

The access steps from the wall to the beach have not been put down yet due to the high water level and lack of beach. The steps needed repair this year which Gary took care of We will reimburse Gary for his expenses. Thanks to Gary, Lisa, Ryan and Ron for keeping the steps in top shape. Hopefully, we will be able to lower the steps this summer once the water reseeds.

Yearly Budget

Paul presented a budget for this year and minimum needed is about $3800 to cover all the common expenses.

 

Summer Projects

Paul addressed projects from last year. The deck project was approved for $1550 and total expenses for the deck ended up being just over $2000 due to extra materials and expenses. However, on the positive side the step staining came in about $130 less and snow plowing was a little less than anticipated.

 

Perimeter Lighting

Dean had a question regarding the lighting. We pay for lights on our streets and one in the restaurant parking lot.

 

Sewer Costs

Dean requested an explanation regarding the money spent to unplug the sewer. Vice President Al Felix explained that he was here and when he flushed to toilet the sewers were all backed up. f lis shower drain was also plugged so he called a Rota Rooter. Sewers were clogged by grease. Please he mindful of what is put down our drains. Also, if you have renters please ask they be careful what they put dispose of in the drain.

 

Water Lines

There was further discussion regarding our aged water lines. Paul said the cost to replace would be between $30,000 - $40,000 dollars. With new standards we would have to install fire hydrants in addition to new water lines. Al and Paul mentioned that he thinks that the water lines on the east road may have been replaced when the restaurant was being remodeled (back in Lou's Billow Beach days - Lou Dehelion). Al said back then we paid for the sewer but never saw any bill for the lines. Gary mentioned that Environmental Services may be able to advise if the lines have been replaced on the east road by the restaurant.

Old Business

Snow Plowing

Paul H. opened up a discussion regarding snow plowing. Currently, BBA averages approximately $1000-$1200 per year for plowing. Paul Shagnot paid $10/push for the east road plowing last winter. There are six residents that reside year round and several do not contribute anything towards this benefit. Non-paying residents take quite a toll on the operation; approximately 30% of our money is spent on snow removal. This is a discussion to be continued and possibly re-visit after dues are paid for 2015.

Lisa mentioned that we should maybe check with our insurance company to see if there is any type of requirement for BBA to provide access on the private roads during the winter.

 

Northeast Road Concrete

Dean provided an update on the northeast road possible project that's been discussed over the past few years. He sent a letter to the 7 residents to get feedback. He received 3 responses. To do the road would be approximately 10 x 240 feet for concrete road; The estimated cost would be approximately $12,000

However, 7 feet of this would require an estimate from the restaurant property owners. Gary had a contractor review the project and recommendation was to stay with slag. In order to do it with concrete would require approximately 2 feet of excavation.

Ron mentioned that before we do more gravel to look into mixing Ready Mix in with the gravel which makes it a more stable surface. This will be an on-going project and discussion.

New Business

Wall Railing

Suzi Hruska is concerned about safety along the wall and would like a railing. We will look into options that would provide some additional safety along the wall.

 

Annual Dues

A brief discussion was held concerning annual dues of $250. All members attending agreed to maintain the dues at the current level.

 

Deck Extension

Ron Zelenak motioned to explore and spend up to $2,000 to expand the deck to the west side. Was approved unanimously

 

Rodent Removal

An invoice was presented to BBA for $100 from Rollie Eldred for a rodent removal. Upon review, this was unanimously decided that removal of animal infestations from private cottages is not the Association's responsibility.
